FRIDAY's Final Brief
FRIDAY: "Boss, here's my honest read after analyzing the data..."

The Android job market in 2026 is brutal but beatable. Most candidates fail for one reason: depth. They know the surface. They can write a ViewModel. They've heard of Hilt. But when an interviewer asks "why does viewModelScope cancel when the ViewModel is cleared?" or "what makes a composable unstable?" — they go silent.

You don't need to know everything. You need to know your core topics — Coroutines, Compose, Hilt, MVVM — so deeply that you can explain them to a 10-year-old or defend them to a Google engineer. That's the bar.

The data also shows something interesting: the market rewards articulate engineers. The candidates who get offers aren't always the most technically brilliant. They're the ones who explain tradeoffs clearly, admit what they don't know honestly, and show genuine curiosity.

Walk in with depth. Walk out with an offer.
