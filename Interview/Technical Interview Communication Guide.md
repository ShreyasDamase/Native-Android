# Technical Interview Communication Guide

## Words & Phrases to Use and Avoid for Clear Technical Explanations

---

## ✅ POWER WORDS TO USE

### **Confidence & Authority**

|Use This|Why It Works|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**"I developed..."**|Shows ownership|"I developed the authentication system using JWT"|
|**"I implemented..."**|Demonstrates action|"I implemented caching to reduce load time by 40%"|
|**"Based on my experience..."**|Establishes credibility|"Based on my experience with Node.js, I recommend..."|
|**"I recommend..."**|Shows confidence in technical judgment|"I recommend using Redis for this use case because..."|
|**"The approach I took was..."**|Clear methodology|"The approach I took was to first analyze the bottleneck..."|

### **Technical Precision**

|Use This|Why It Works|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**"Specifically..."**|Adds clarity|"Specifically, I used React hooks for state management"|
|**"To clarify..."**|Shows thoughtfulness|"To clarify, hoisting applies to function declarations, not expressions"|
|**"Let me explain..."**|Takes control|"Let me explain how closures work in JavaScript"|
|**"In other words..."**|Ensures understanding|"In other words, async/await is syntactic sugar for promises"|
|**"For example..."**|Makes concepts concrete|"For example, when you call a function before declaration..."|

### **Structured Thinking**

|Use This|Why It Works|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**"There are three key differences..."**|Shows organization|"There are three key differences: scope, hoisting, and mutability"|
|**"First... Second... Finally..."**|Clear progression|"First, var is function-scoped. Second, it's hoisted..."|
|**"The main advantage is..."**|Prioritizes information|"The main advantage is better memory management"|
|**"This works because..."**|Shows deep understanding|"This works because JavaScript moves declarations to the top"|
|**"The reason is..."**|Explains rationale|"The reason is that arrow functions don't bind their own 'this'"|

### **Problem-Solving**

|Use This|Why It Works|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**"I debugged this by..."**|Shows methodology|"I debugged this by checking the console and using breakpoints"|
|**"I optimized..."**|Demonstrates improvement focus|"I optimized the query by adding an index"|
|**"I refactored..."**|Shows code quality awareness|"I refactored the component to use custom hooks"|
|**"The solution was..."**|Result-oriented|"The solution was to implement memoization"|
|**"I analyzed..."**|Shows analytical thinking|"I analyzed the time complexity and chose a more efficient algorithm"|

### **Collaboration**

|Use This|Why It Works|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**"I collaborated with..."**|Shows teamwork|"I collaborated with the backend team to design the API"|
|**"We determined..."**|Inclusive approach|"We determined that TypeScript would improve code quality"|
|**"I learned..."**|Shows growth mindset|"I learned about Web Workers when optimizing performance"|
|**"I researched..."**|Shows initiative|"I researched different state management solutions"|

## ❌ WORDS & PHRASES TO AVOID

### **Weak/Diminishing Language**

|Avoid|Why It's Bad|Say Instead|
|---|---|---|
|**"Just"**|Diminishes your work|"I just handled the frontend" → "I developed the entire frontend"|
|**"Only"**|Minimizes achievements|"I only worked on..." → "I was responsible for..."|
|**"Kind of"**|Shows uncertainty|"It's kind of like..." → "It functions similarly to..."|
|**"Sort of"**|Lacks precision|"It's sort of asynchronous" → "It's asynchronous"|
|**"I think"**|Sounds uncertain|"I think hoisting..." → "Hoisting works by..."|
|**"Maybe"**|Too hesitant|"Maybe we could use..." → "We could use..." or "I recommend..."|
|**"Pretty much"**|Imprecise|"It's pretty much done" → "It's complete" or "It's 90% complete"|

### **Filler Words**

|Avoid|Why It's Bad|Alternative|
|---|---|---|
|**"Um"**|Shows nervousness|Pause silently instead|
|**"Like"**|Unprofessional|Remove entirely or use "such as"|
|**"You know"**|Assumes knowledge|Be explicit: "As you may know..."|
|**"So"** (overused)|Filler|Use sparingly, or start with your point|
|**"Actually"**|Can sound condescending|Remove or use "In fact"|
|**"Basically"**|Can sound patronizing|Remove or be more specific|

### **Vague/Generic Terms**

|Avoid|Why It's Bad|Say Instead|
|---|---|---|
|**"Stuff"**|Too casual|"Features," "functionality," "components"|
|**"Things"**|Imprecise|Name specific items|
|**"A lot"**|Vague quantity|"Approximately 50," "multiple," "several"|
|**"Amazing"**|Overused buzzword|"High-performing," "efficient," "optimized"|
|**"Awesome"**|Too casual|"Effective," "robust," "scalable"|
|**"Cool"**|Unprofessional|"Innovative," "elegant," "efficient"|

### **Negative Language**

|Avoid|Why It's Bad|Say Instead|
|---|---|---|
|**"I don't know"**|Shows unpreparedness|"That's a great question. Let me think..." or "I'd need to research that"|
|**"I can't"**|Too negative|"I haven't worked with that yet, but I can learn"|
|**"I hate"**|Too strong/negative|"I prefer" or "I find X more effective than Y"|
|**"That's wrong"**|Confrontational|"I see it differently" or "Another approach would be..."|
|**"Problem"**|Negative framing|"Challenge" or "opportunity for improvement"|
|**"Weakness"**|Self-defeating|"Area for growth" or "skill I'm developing"|

### **Apologetic Language**

|Avoid|Why It's Bad|Say Instead|
|---|---|---|
|**"Sorry"** (unnecessary)|Shows lack of confidence|Only apologize for actual mistakes|
|**"I'm not sure but..."**|Undermines credibility|"Based on what I know..."|
|**"This might be wrong..."**|Pre-emptive defeat|State your answer, then add "Please correct me if I'm mistaken"|
|**"I hope that makes sense"**|Shows insecurity|"Does that answer your question?" or "Would you like me to elaborate?"|

### **Overconfident/Exaggerated**

|Avoid|Why It's Bad|Say Instead|
|---|---|---|
|**"I'm an expert"**|Can backfire if tested|"I have extensive experience in..."|
|**"I'm a master at..."**|Sounds arrogant|"I'm proficient in..." or "I specialize in..."|
|**"I know everything about..."**|Impossible claim|"I have deep knowledge of..."|
|**"That's easy"**|Dismissive|"I'm comfortable with that"|
|**"Perfectionist"**|Cliché red flag|"I value code quality and attention to detail"|

### **Passive Voice (When Explaining Your Work)**

|Avoid|Why It's Bad|Say Instead|
|---|---|---|
|**"It was decided..."**|Unclear ownership|"I decided..." or "We decided..."|
|**"The code was written..."**|Passive|"I wrote the code..."|
|**"Mistakes were made"**|Evasive|"I made a mistake and learned..."|

## 🎯 THE 3 C's OF TECHNICAL COMMUNICATION

### 1. **CLEAR** - Easy to Understand

- Use simple, precise language
- Define technical terms when first mentioned
- Avoid jargon unless interviewer uses it first
- Structure your explanation logically

**Example:** ❌ "The thing with var is it does this hoisting stuff" ✅ "var declarations are hoisted to the top of their scope and initialized as undefined"

### 2. **CONCISE** - Brief but Complete

- Get to the point quickly
- Remove unnecessary words
- Don't over-explain unless asked
- Use concrete examples

**Example:** ❌ "So basically what I'm trying to say is that like, arrow functions are kind of different because they don't really have their own 'this' binding, you know what I mean?" ✅ "Arrow functions inherit 'this' from their parent scope rather than binding their own"

### 3. **CONFIDENT** - Assured but Humble

- State facts directly
- Admit when you don't know something
- Show willingness to learn
- Avoid hedging language

**Example:** ❌ "I think maybe hoisting might possibly move declarations up, but I'm not 100% sure" ✅ "Hoisting moves declarations to the top of their scope. The behavior differs between var, let, and const"

## 📋 COMMUNICATION STRUCTURE FOR TECHNICAL EXPLANATIONS

### **The PREP Method**

1. **Point** - State your main answer directly
2. **Reason** - Explain why/how it works
3. **Example** - Give a concrete code example
4. **Point** - Restate concisely

**Example:**

```
Q: "What's the difference between var and let?"

Point: "The main difference is scope - var is function-scoped while let is block-scoped."

Reason: "This means var is accessible throughout the entire function, but let is only accessible within the block where it's declared, like inside an if statement or loop."

Example: "For instance, if I declare var x inside an if block, I can access x outside that block. But with let y, trying to access y outside the block throws a ReferenceError."

Point: "So in summary: var gives function scope, let gives block scope, which provides better control over variable accessibility."
```

## 🚀 QUICK REFERENCE CARD

### ✅ DO USE:

- "I developed/implemented/built..."
- "The reason is..."
- "For example..."
- "Based on my experience..."
- "Let me clarify..."
- Concrete technical terms
- Active voice
- Structured explanations

### ❌ DON'T USE:

- "Just/only/kind of/sort of"
- "Um/like/you know"
- "Stuff/things/a lot"
- "I think/maybe/probably"
- "Sorry" (unnecessarily)
- "I'm an expert"
- Vague generalizations
- Passive voice

## 💡 PRO TIPS

1. **Pause Instead of Filler**: Take a breath instead of saying "um" or "like"
    
2. **Ask for Clarification**: "Could you clarify what aspect you'd like me to focus on?" (Better than guessing)
    
3. **Check Understanding**: "Does that answer your question?" or "Would you like me to elaborate on any part?"
    
4. **Admit Unknowns Confidently**: "I haven't worked with that specific technology, but I'm familiar with similar concepts in X"
    
5. **Use the Interviewer's Language**: If they say "function hoisting," use that term. If they say "lifting," mirror that.
    
6. **Pace Yourself**: Speak at a moderate pace. Fast talking can signal nervousness.
    
7. **Eliminate Hedging**: Compare:
    
    - ❌ "I sort of think that maybe it might work like..."
    - ✅ "It works like this..."
8. **Own Your Experience**:
    
    - ❌ "I was just part of a team that..."
    - ✅ "As part of the team, I was responsible for..."
